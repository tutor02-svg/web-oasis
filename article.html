Understanding Depression

Depression is a common but serious mood disorder that negatively affects how you feel, think, and act. It can lead to a variety of emotional and physical problems and can decrease your ability to function at work and at home.

People experiencing depression may feel persistent sadness, hopelessness, or emptiness. They often lose interest in activities once enjoyed, have trouble sleeping or concentrating, and may experience changes in appetite or energy levels.

Causes of Depression

  • Genetic factors or family history of mental illness.
  • Prolonged stress, trauma, or difficult life experiences.
  • Chemical imbalances in the brain affecting mood regulation.
  • Substance abuse or chronic physical illness.

How to Cope & Seek Help

Recovery is possible with the right support. Talking to trusted friends, joining support groups, and seeking professional help from psychologists or counselors are important steps. Regular exercise, balanced nutrition, and mindfulness practices can also improve mood and mental health.

Common Symptoms

  • Flashbacks, nightmares, or severe anxiety related to the traumatic event.
  • Emotional numbness or avoidance of reminders of the trauma.
  • Negative changes in mood, thoughts, or perception of safety.
  • Being easily startled or having difficulty sleeping or concentrating.
